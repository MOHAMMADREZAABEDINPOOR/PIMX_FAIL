#1
WeWork
Real Estate Tech
WeWork revolutionized modern coworking but suffered from an unsustainable asset-liability mismatch. It borrowed long (signing 15 to 20-year leases) and leased short (flexible monthly subscriptions). When economic shifts reduced occupancies, the locked-in real estate obligations remained. Combined with erratic administration and wasteful side-ventures (like WeGrow and WeLive), WeWork depleted billions in capital before filing for Chapter 11 bankruptcy reorganization in late 2023.

Investigative reporting by the Wall Street Journal exposed the deception, regulators voided results, and founder Elizabeth Holmes was convicted of fraud. The company dissolved in 2018 as a textbook case of technical fraud meeting weak regulatory oversight.
